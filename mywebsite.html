Festival Guide

Celebrate Festivals In Traditional Way

Festivals

  • Diwali
  • Navaratri
  • Karthika Deepam
  • Onam
  • Ganesh Chathurthi
  • Makar Sankranthi

Diwali

Previous Next
  1. Start with "Pooja Room"
  2. Get going with "Genda Phool"
  3. String up lights outside your home
  4. Deck up the door
  5. Follow the "Muhurat"
  6. Ramp up on the best Rangoli designs

Navaratri

Previous Next
  1. Light the lamp and incense sticks.
  2. Offer some flowers to Ma Durga and decorate the picture or idol with vermilion, chandan and turmeric paste.
  3. Every day during the puja, sprinkle some water on the barley seeds you had sown.
  4. Offer some interesting dishes you specially prepared for the puja.
  5. Do Arati to Maa Durga.
  6. Distribute the Prasad to the invitees and members of the family.
  7. Do this puja everyday over the nine days of Navratri.

Karthika Deepam

Previous Next
  1. Clean your home to prepare it for the festival.
  2. Hang up seasonal decorations to prepare for the celebration.
  3. Draw Rangoli near your door to welcome the Goddess Lakshmi.
  4. Place clay lamps on or near the kolams and light them.
  5. Light colorful lamps and candles throughout your home.
  6. Meditate with the success of your family members in mind.
  7. Visit "Tiruvannamalai hill" to see the lighting of the lamp if you're in India.
  8. Walk around the town to view the decorations and lights.

Onam

Previous Next
  1. Visit the temples on the first day.
  2. Take part in a procession if you are near Thrippunithura.
  3. Begin to create the Pookalam.
  4. Clean your house on day 2.
  5. Hit the market on day 3.
  6. Start preparing the sadhya on day 4.
  7. Exchange gifts with loved ones on day 6.
  8. Visit other families in your community on day 7.
  9. Welcome the statues of Mahabali and Vamana on day 8.
  10. Make the final preparations for the sadya meal on day 9.

Ganesh Chathurthi

Previous Next
  1. Buy a clay idol of Lord Ganesha.
  2. Gather other items you'll need to gain Ganesha's favor.
  3. Light your diya.
  4. Offer ladoos, modaks, or other treats to Ganesha.
  5. Burn incense as an offering.
  6. Put garland around the neck of the idol.
  7. Repeat one of Ganesha's mantras.
  8. Close your eyes and summon the image of Lord Ganesha in your mind.
  9. Speak to Lord Ganesha, once you've visualized him.
  10. Apply tilak to the idol, yourself, and to others in attendance.
  11. Offer the "Prasadam" to those who do attended the Puja.

Makar Sankranthi

Previous Next
  1. Pray to the Sun god after taking a bath in the morning.
  2. Donate clothes to the poor and needy people on Makar Sakranti.
  3. Make some puris (Fried small chapatti) in mustard oil and feed them to street dogs.
  4. On this day, you must distribute two equal parts of black sesame seeds.
  5. Prepare Khichdi from Urad dal and offer as bhog to Lord Shani. You can consume khichdi after offering bhog only.
  6. It is a believed that people should spend maximum time under the sunlight to prevent off any infection.
  7. Enjoy the festival of kites and remember, “Kites rise highest against the wind and not with the wind”.
  8. Worship "Gomaadha" on 2nd day of Pongal.
